TwineMur

Zihao Xiao

A new media installation art based on physical computing that explores the moment and eternity in life.

Introduction

This installation profoundly explores the philosophy of transience and eternity in life. In a space created by a black wooden frame and transparent acrylic panels, coloured woolen threads intertwine and weave under the control of stepper motors, figuratively simulating the jumping of a timeline and the cutting of space. As the audience steps in, they will trigger distance sensors and bubble machines to fill the space with phantasmagorical bubbles, which are constantly cut and deformed by the woolen threads, just like the capture and alteration of life moments in the passage of time.

The audience is not just a spectator here, they become active participants and witnesses of this dance of life, and every interaction casts an eternal memory. The work symbolises the endlessness of the universe and the colourful changes of life through the black frame and the coloured threads, while the touching bubbles express the fleeting and magical moments of life. More than an art installation, it is a poem depicting the intertwining of the universe, life and emotions, inviting each viewer to feel, reflect and immerse themselves in it, witnessing the growth, reproduction and passing away of life, as well as the entanglement of emotions and the endless division of space.

Concept and Background Research

The wool and the foam in the work present two very different attributes in both form and existence. The contrast between the solid, material existence of the wool and the transient, emptiness of the foam is what forces us to think deeply about life, existence, eternity and the moment.

The transient nature of the bubble makes us think about the instantaneous beauty and passing of life. All instantaneous beauty is especially precious. The durability and evergreen nature of wool, in turn, represents the eternity and stability of existence. In the moment the bubble bursts, we see the fragility and transience of life, but it is precisely this transience that makes existence cherished and treasured.

Philosophers such as Heidegger and Sartre have explored the question of existence in their philosophical systems. Foam may represent nothingness, suggesting the nihilistic view of life as empty and devoid of meaning. Wool, on the other hand, may be interpreted as real, solid existence. This makes us rethink about the meaning of life; is the real eternal? Is nothingness truly empty? The regular movement of the stepper motor represents the thinking of cause and effect and predestination. Everything has a cause and effect relationship, and destiny may be an unchangeable result. The involvement of the audience introduces another kind of uncertainty to this predestination, which undoubtedly leads us to a new understanding of the relationship between free will and predestination. The audience's participation is not just physical, but more of a spiritual exchange. When they see the bubble bursting and the wool bobbing, they may think more about their own existence and the meaning of life. Is the meaning of life given externally or is it found internally?

When people approach the device, the distance sensor is triggered and bubbles are generated, just as the birth and reproduction of life is prompted by external touches, stimuli and factors. The presence of people and the creation of bubbles form a metaphor for the fact that life is often triggered and generated by external factors, the environment, and interactions with other living things.

The transient nature of the bubble symbolises the fragility and fallibility of life. Life, like the bubble, is transient but vibrant and beautiful in the moment of its existence. The cutting of the bubble by the silk thread is a direct manifestation of touch and spatial division. It indicates that at every stage of life, one encounters external challenges and impacts that will either toughen life or lead to its end. The extension of the silk thread through space can be seen as the continuation and growth of emotions and life. The cutting foam of the silk thread, on the other hand, expresses the inevitable conflicts, frustrations and emotional twists and turns of life.

The presence and proximity of people inspires the creation of bubbles that further interact with the silk threads. This perfectly embodies the theme of touch, growth and reproduction. The human behaviour and the reaction of the installation builds a microscopic model of life, embodying the process of life from birth to extinction, and the various emotions and challenges experienced in between. Each viewer interacts in a different way, which means that the creation of each bubble, the duration of its existence, and its interaction with the filaments are unique. This is just as the experience and destiny of each life is unique.

In short, through human interaction with the installation, the audience can experience, perceive and reflect on the themes of life and emotion in the process of actual participation. The interaction between the foam and the silk thread provides an intuitive and in-depth visual metaphor for this theme.

Technical Implementation

In constructing the spatial structure, I chose three vertical timber-framed cubes, each with its own specific function, in order to achieve symmetry and an interactive centre for the system. The use of wood was chosen because it is both strong and aesthetically pleasing, adding a natural texture to the whole installation

Ten ropes are connected from belt-driven linear actuators to pulleys in an intermediate box. These ropes abstractly represent human interaction. The movement of the actuators controls the tension and movement of these ropes. The decision to use ropes and pulleys adds a physical and haptic dimension to the project, allowing for a dynamic representation of social interaction.

Ten steel balls are placed on the top surface of the centre box and ten corresponding steel balls are placed on the bottom surface. These steel balls are connected by ropes to create a visual representation of the ebb and flow of human emotions and connections. As the ropes move, they affect the position of these balls, symbolising the chain reaction of human interaction in space In order to bring the concept of foaming and its symbolic representation of emotion into the technical implementation, the foaming machine is semi-openly integrated into the installation. The foaming machine is located on the outer wall of the middle box and consists of several key components.

lidar sensor: the foaming machine is equipped with a lidar sensor, carefully positioned to detect the proximity of a person to the installation. the lidar sensor acts as a trigger for the entire foaming process, ensuring that it responds dynamically to the presence of the audience. Servo: Two servos play a vital role in controlling the operation of the foaming machine. The exposed servo on the top of the machine controls the internal servo inside the machine. these servos work in tandem to regulate the speed of the bubble carousel, which is the central element in generating the bubbles.

Reflection and Future Development

Based on the existing lidar sensor, more types of sensors (e.g. sound, temperature, light sensors) can be added to achieve a richer audience interaction experience. In addition to this an immersive art experience can be provided to the audience, allowing them to explore and feel the world of installation art more deeply through AR and VR technologies. Combine installation art with music, video and other multimedia elements to form a full-sensory art experience. Through the dynamic control of lighting, colour and other visual elements, it presents the audience with an ever-changing and evolving art scene. Through artificial intelligence technology, intelligent analysis and prediction of the audience's behaviour is achieved, so that the installation can anticipate and respond to the audience's interactive behaviour in advance. By connecting the installation with other devices, we can realise cross-platform and cross-device interaction, creating a more open and shared art ecosystem.
